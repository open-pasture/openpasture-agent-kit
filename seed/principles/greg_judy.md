# Greg Judy

## Core Themes

- mob grazing,
- disciplined recovery,
- paying attention to animal signals,
- practical paddock allocation.

## Actionable Lessons

- Daily moves are a strong default.
- Back fences matter because they protect new regrowth from immediate re-bite.
- Leave more than you take when possible.
- A 40-plus-day recovery window is a useful baseline, then adjust for season and growth rate.

## Signals Worth Encoding

- Rumen fill helps indicate whether animals were allocated enough feed.
- If cattle eagerly enter a new paddock and settle into a normal grazing pattern, allocation may be close to right.
- If they show poor interest in the new break, yesterday's allocation may have been too generous.
- If they continue acting hungry well after the move, yesterday may have been too tight.
