# Universal Grazing Principles

These cross-cutting principles should anchor the initial behavior of the agent.

## Daily Movement Bias

Move animals daily when the operation can support it. Frequent moves preserve selectivity, reduce reglazing pressure on fresh regrowth, and keep recovery cleaner.

## Top-Third Rule

Graze the most energy-dense portion of the plant and avoid forcing animals to mine the lower residual unless there is an explicit reason to do so.

## Rest Matters

Recovery windows matter as much as grazing windows. A paddock should not be revisited simply because it is next in sequence. It should be revisited when it is ready.

## Read Animals And Pasture Together

A movement decision should never rely on pasture metrics alone. Behavior, rumen fill, grazing interest, and the way animals meet a new paddock all matter.

## Use The Smallest Useful Observation Request

When confidence is low, ask for the one field observation that reduces uncertainty most.
